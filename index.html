DesignForAI



Symposium


The TU Delft Industrial Design Engineering (IDE) faculty is hosting the premier European event on Design for AI, October 14, 2022. This one-day symposium features keynote speakers, workshops and networking opportunities that will provide insight for those involved in the design and deployment of AI systems and partnerships.

In a world full of artificial intelligence, how do we design spaces and opportunities for human growth and wellbeing? How to build safe and meaningful relationships between AI and people? We happily invite you to this conversation at IDE's first Design for AI — Are we designing the AI we need? symposium on Friday 14th of October. In collaboration with the TU Delft AI Initiative and Mondai — House of AI, we welcome internationally renowned speakers Elizabeth Churchill, John Zimmermann, Matt Jones and Caroline Sinders. During the day we also provide workshops, such as from the CHI NL Community. Tickets are available via: https://design4aisympo.eventbrite.nl/. Don’t miss out on the Early Bird tickets! Limited spots, first come, first serve.

Keynote Speakers

Elizabeth Churchill

Director of User Experience at Google

Ways in which smart assistants can help software developers

Elizabeth Churchill is a Elizabeth Churchill is a Director of UX at Google and Executive Vice President of the Association of Computing Machinery (ACM). With a background in psychology, Artificial Intelligence, and Cognitive Science, she draws on social, computer, engineering, and data sciences to create innovative end-user applications and services. She has built research teams at Google, eBay, Yahoo, PARC, and FujiXerox. In 2016, she received a Citris-Banatao Institute Award Athena Award for Women in Technology for her Executive Leadership. She has been named one of the top women leaders in UX over the last several years. Her current focus is on the design of effective developer tooling.

John Zimmerman

Tang Family Professor of Artificial Intelligence and Human-Computer InteractionCMU Human-Computer Interaction Institute School of Design

Why AI initiatives have an 85% failure rate
A lack of ideation, a failure to search for opportunities where mediocre performing AI still adds value, and failure to assess and select project ideas based on AI performance risks, FATE risks, and value to customers and service providers. I believe there is a huge opportunity for design to scaffold ideation and project selection; to facilitate communication between data scientists, software developers, PMs, and domain experts; and to create boundary objects that allow better communication and understanding in the earliest stages of an AI innovation process.

John Zimmerman researches human-AI interaction, human-robot interaction, and innovation with analytics and AI. For more than twenty years, he has designed novel, intelligent systems ranging from one of the first TV show recommenders to a crowdsourced, transit arrival system to a decision support tool for implanting mechanical hearts to a system that keeps parents from forgetting to pick up their children. He’s published more than 150 papers, is a member of the ACM CHI Academy, and while working for Philips, he invented the way everyone scrolls on their smartphone. He teaches courses in service design, lean startup, and on the design of AI products and services.

John is the co-author of the book: Design Research through Practice.

Matt Jones

Principal Designer at Moixa

Optometrists, Octopii, Rubber Ducks & Centaurs
Designing for (and with) systems featuring AI has many emerging metaphors - but how do they match to the materiality of the systems they represent? Do they aid legibility or widen the mystery gap? Looking back at projects I've worked on, I'll examine how they've informed my practice of design in the field - and how they land in the world

Matt Jones is Principal Designer at Lunar Energy, a smart battery company enabling decarbonisation. He has been designing digital products and services since 1995 at organizations such as BBC and Nokia. From 2009-2013 he was a principal at BERG, a design and invention company in London that had projects exhibited in MoMA, and products featured in the FT, FastCompany, Wired and Marvel comics. In 2013 he moved to New York City to join Google Creative Lab where projects he worked on included Google Expeditions and Sunroof. In 2016 he returned to London as part of Google’s Research division, where he worked on AI concepts for hardware and services until 2021. He originally studied Architecture, has written on interaction design (amongst other things) for 20 years at petafloptimism.com and has taught design at the RCA, Goldsmiths, SVA, Umea and CIID.

Caroline Sinders

Critical designer, researcher, and artist

Talk Title
Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet

Caroline Sinders is an award winning critical designer, researcher, and artist. For the past few years, she has been examining the intersections of AI, intersectional justice, systems design, harm, and politics in digital conversational spaces and technology platforms. She has worked with the United Nations, Amnesty International, IBM Watson, the Wikimedia Foundation, and others. Sinders has held numerous fellowships, such as with the Harvard Kennedy School, Google’s PAIR (People and Artificial Intelligence Research group), Ars Electronica’s AI Lab, the Weizenbaum Institute, the Mozilla Foundation and Ars Electronica. Her work has been featured in the Tate Exchange in Tate Modern, Victoria and Albert Museum, MoMA PS1, Wired and many others.
